Project Introduction

Colorful Memory is a cognition training device for old people, especially for those who are in the Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) stage. It could perform cognition training through a delightful game which requires old people to memorize the color sequence as well as to move their bodies to tap the lights. Therefore, the risk of developing into Alzheimer's Disease (AD) will be largely reduced.




Project information

  • Type :A group project for the China-Us Young Maker Competition
  • Award :The first prize of China-Us Young Maker Competition, Shanghai
  • Team Member :Xiaoyu Sun, Jing Zhao, Jingcheng Li, Qianwen Zhang, Zhoujun Ma
  • My Role :Interaction Design, Literature Review, Software Development
  • Date :Jue 2016


Background


We made this device in a 48-hour maker competition. We chose to design a cognition training device because two of our teammates' grandfathers have Alzheimer's Disease, so we wanted to help our families with our knowledge and skills.


Nowadays, the aging of the population has become a severe problem, while China's population is aging at unprecedented levels. The statistical figure shows that there are more than 127 million old people all over the world. Alzheimer's Disease (AD) is the 4th fatal disease among old people.

Since the present medical technology can not cure AD, the MCI period is the best time to apply the therapeutic interventions to slow the progression of the AD. But the primary treatments now still have many problems.


Apart from the side-effects or being too boring, these treatments make the old people with MCI feel they are patients while the patients are reluctant to be recognized as patients. As a consequence, we chose to design a device that would be suitable for all the old people instead of especially for individuals who suffer from MCI.



Brainstorm


Our group went through several rounds of brainstorm to figure out what kind of product would help old people most delightfully. Finally, we decided to combine body movement and to memorize color sequence together in that colors are the most common and most essential information for people to remember.



Product Design & Interaction Design


Considering that old people tend to have weak eyesight and they are not interested in digital devices our gro decided to create an interaction device without digital screens.

For the product design, we got inspirations from the traditional Chinese drums. The interaction model of our design developed from the way of beating drums. The movements in this game are aimed at training their limbs. The position of the separate parts is carefully designed to ensure the safety as well as the training intensity.


Then I designed the user flow as below. The device is like an interactive game machine. To make it easier for old people to play, the game is simplified to only remembering color sequences and tap the device. The color sequences will become longer as the level up.


From this, we developed the game logic for our software architecture.


Development


We used the Arduino101 Development Board and also other modules like the pressure sensor, vibration sensor, RGB LED and so on. Our aim is to achieve the most sophisticated function by using the most basic hardware. As for software architecture, we use the finite state machine to implement the game logic to make it more extendable.



Final Prototype


Although we still have some pities, it is an exciting experience that we built this prototype in 48 hours.



Future Work


The device could be placed at the rehabilitation center, community square, the park and so forth. And the product could also be extended to the field of child cognition training. We could add PK mode and other interesting incentive mechanisms to encourage more people to take part in the game. Besides, the device could be connected to the smart wearable device to get more accurate data.
