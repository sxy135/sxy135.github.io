What is KMA?

KMA is short for Key Measures Analyzer. It is a powerful internal tool for analysts to understand the potential success of a product through comparisons. The analysts compose competitive sets of the study data and then run comparisons to get the prediction results.


Why do we need comparisons in KMA? Because we can't tell if the raw data is good or bad without context.

For example, if a company wants to launch a new type of coke. They might reach out to Nielsen and do marketing researches. In the end, they may get a potential customer satisfaction rate of 80%. But what does 80% indicate? We can't tell without comparing this with other data. If we could know the average satisfaction rate of coke is about 70%, then we can defer that product is likely to have success in the market. Of course, comparisons in KMA are much more complicated than this, but this can reflect how KMA works in general.
