Project Overview

  • About : This is a redesign project of a complicated data analytics system of Nielsen called KMA.
    We managed to integrate the old KMA system into the new Nielsen services platform.

    We also fixed the usability issues of the current system to reduce the learning efforts and to improve the efficiency.
  • My Role : Lead UX Designer - participated in user research, product strategy, prototyping, and user testing
  • Timeline : Sep 2019 - Present, plan to launch around Sep 2020
  • Impact : Largely reduced training time, 95.8% improved user satisfaction rate, and successful integration to the Nielsen application platform




Project Timeline


This project has three phases. We started with the background research, and then we tried to define the problems by walking through the current system and getting initial feedback from the user panel.

Then, I had three rounds of design iteration to evolve from wireframe to high-fidelity prototype. In between each round, I would present my design to the product manager, the design team, the development team, and the user panel to get helpful feedback.

At present, we are in the third phase of developing the system, and I am supporting the development team to finalize screens for MVP.





What is KMA?

KMA is short for Key Measures Analyzer. It is a powerful internal tool for analysts to understand the potential success of a product through comparisons. The analysts compose competitive sets of the study data and then run comparisons to get the prediction results.


Why do we need comparisons in KMA? Because we can't tell if the raw data is good or bad without context.

For example, if a company wants to launch a new drink. They might do the marketing research get a potential customer satisfaction rate of 80%. But what does 80% indicate? We can't tell without comparing this with other data. If we could know the average satisfaction rate of all drinks is about 70%, then we can infer that product is likely to have success in the market. Of course, comparisons in KMA are much more complicated than this, but this can reflect how KMA works.

Impacts


1. Consistency with Nielsen design system

One significant achievement of this project is that we successfully integrated the old KMA system into the new Nielsen application platform called "Nielsen Studio." To keep the design consistent with the current platform, I studied and then applied the components and patterns while designing.

2. Increased user satisfaction & Foreseeable improvement in efficiency, learnability, and visibility

According to the survey results that we have collected among 27 users who have seen our final designs, we were surprised to find out that the overall satisfaction score rose from 2.4 to 4.7, which means an approximately 95.8% improvement in terms of user satisfaction.

Also, from the word cloud of their answers to the question of how they feel about the new design, we can see most users think that the new design is intuitive, user-friendly, and efficient.

In addition, based on our conversation with the managers, they think the visibility of the projects has improved a lot. They could monitor the progress of projects from the homepage without going deeper into the system.

Reflection


1. Design with Constraints

The first challenge for me was to design with many constraints like time and resources budget from the product manager, the current design system of Nielsen, and the technical complexities from the developments. To balance these constraints, I tried to involve all stakeholders (PM, design team, dev team, and users) in the iteration process and revisit the design after listening to all their opinions. However, despite different voices from all stakeholders, it's my responsibility to determine the priority of all these factors and make the final design decision.

2. Thinking Out of the Box

Another interesting finding from this project is that the more I learned about the current system, the more I am getting used to its information architecture and wireframe. It was hard for me to have a completely new idea at first. To solve this problem, I tried to think from a new perspective. I started to think about what users want the most from KMA, and then went through all the necessary steps users had to do to get this result. This helped me to rethink the end-to-end flow of the whole system and see opportunities to improve.
